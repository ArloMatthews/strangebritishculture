BRITISH FESTIVITIES

There are many events, traditional and sporadic, that are celebrated in England and may seem a little odd. There are many holidays, anniversaries, and other occasions which call for these obscure traditions to be celebrated. These include special ceremonies, dances, displays and mixtures of all of them.

Jack in the Green: A type of Morris Dancing where people entirely cover themselves to resemble hedges and march

Lewes Fireworks: on November 5th, people crowd the streets holding burning objects and setting off fireworks

Maypole Dancing: a highly choreographed ribbon folk dance around a maypole to welcome the arrival of spring

Morris Dancing: a big dance in feathery costumes and face paint that was originally taken from the Spanish

Whittlesea Straw Bear Festival: a performer dresses up in a costume made of straw and parades the streets with dancers

Weighing the Mayor: in Buckinghamshire, mayors are weighed at each end of their term to see if they've been taking advantage of the town's money
